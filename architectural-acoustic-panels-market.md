<p><p>Architectural acoustic panels are designed to improve sound quality within a space by reducing noise levels and controlling reverberation. There are three main types of acoustic panels: Acoustic membranes, Resonators Panel, and Porous Material Panel. Acoustic membranes use a thin material to control sound reflection and absorption. Resonators Panel are designed to absorb sound through vibrating panels or perforated surfaces. Porous Material Panel use materials like foam, fabric, or fiberglass to absorb sound waves and reduce echo. Each type of panel offers unique benefits for improving acoustics in different environments.</p></p>
