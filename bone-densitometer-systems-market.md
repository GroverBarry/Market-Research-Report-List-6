<p><p>Bone densitometer systems are used to measure the density of bones, which can indicate the risk of fractures and osteoporosis. There are two main types of bone densitometry systems: axial and peripheral. Axial bone densitometry measures density in the spine and hip, while peripheral bone densitometry measures density in the forearm, heel, or finger. Both types of systems are important for early detection and monitoring of bone health to prevent the risk of fractures and other bone-related conditions.</p></p>
