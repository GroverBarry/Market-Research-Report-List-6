<p><p>Laboratory Filtration market consists of various types of filtration processes including Ultrafiltration, Microfiltration, Nanofiltration, and Reverse Osmosis. </p><p>Ultrafiltration involves separating suspended solids and solutes from a liquid using membrane filters. Microfiltration is used to remove particles and bacteria from liquids. Nanofiltration is effective in removing divalent ions and organic molecules. Reverse Osmosis is a process where a solvent passes through a semi-permeable membrane to remove ions, molecules, and larger particles from water. Each type of filtration serves a specific purpose in laboratory applications.</p></p>
