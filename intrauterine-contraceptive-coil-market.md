<p><p>Intrauterine contraceptive coils, also known as intrauterine devices (IUDs), come in two main types - hormonal and copper. Hormonal IUDs release hormones to prevent pregnancy by thickening cervical mucus and inhibiting sperm movement. Copper IUDs work by creating an inflammatory response that is toxic to sperm. Both types are highly effective at preventing pregnancy and can be left in place for several years. The market for these devices continues to grow as more women seek long-acting and reversible contraception options.</p></p>
