<p><p>Implantable Defibrillators are devices used to regulate and restore normal heart rhythm in patients at risk of sudden cardiac arrest. There are three main types in the market: Subcutaneous Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillators (S-ICDs), Transvenous Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillators (T-ICDs), and Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy Defibrillators (CRT-D). S-ICDs are placed just under the skin, T-ICDs are inserted through veins into the heart, and CRT-Ds are used for patients with heart failure to improve heart function and prevent sudden cardiac arrest.</p></p>
