<p><p>Automotive safety systems can be categorized into two main types: Active Safety Systems and Passive Safety Systems. Active safety systems are designed to prevent accidents by providing warnings and assisting the driver in maintaining control of the vehicle, such as anti-lock braking systems and electronic stability control. On the other hand, Passive safety systems are focused on mitigating the impact of accidents by reducing the severity of injuries, such as airbags and seat belts. Both types of safety systems play a crucial role in enhancing overall vehicle safety.</p></p>
