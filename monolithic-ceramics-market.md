<p><p>Monolithic ceramics can be classified into two main types: oxides and non-oxides. Oxides are composed primarily of metal and oxygen atoms, such as alumina, silica, and zirconia. They are commonly used for their high strength, hardness, and resistance to corrosion. Non-oxides, on the other hand, consist of carbides, nitrides, and borides, which offer unique properties like high melting points, thermal conductivity, and electrical conductivity. Both types of monolithic ceramics play a vital role in various industries due to their exceptional properties and versatility.</p></p>
