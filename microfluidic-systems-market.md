<p><p>Microfluidic systems are classified into three main types based on the materials used: glass, polymer, and silicon. Glass microfluidic systems offer excellent optical properties and chemical resistance, making them ideal for applications requiring high transparency and biocompatibility. Polymer microfluidic systems are cost-effective and flexible, making them suitable for rapid prototyping and disposable devices. Silicon microfluidic systems are known for their precise microfabrication capabilities and compatibility with standard semiconductor processes, making them ideal for integrating electronics and fluid handling on a single chip.</p></p>
