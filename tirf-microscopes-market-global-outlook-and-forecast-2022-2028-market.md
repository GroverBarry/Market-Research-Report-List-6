The Prism Method involves directing the excitation light through a prism to create an evanescent wave, suitable for imaging close to the surface. On the other hand, the Objective Lens Method uses high numerical aperture objective lenses to achieve total internal reflection, enabling imaging of a specific region close to the surface. Both methods offer unique advantages for different applications in the field of microscopy.</p></p>
