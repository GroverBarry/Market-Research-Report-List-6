<p><p>Granular media filtration is a type of wastewater treatment filter that uses sand or other granular materials to remove suspended solids and impurities from water. This method is effective in removing larger particles and can improve water clarity. Fiber filtration, on the other hand, uses a porous fabric or membrane to physically trap contaminants in the water. This type of filtration is efficient in removing smaller particles and can effectively remove pathogens and other harmful substances from wastewater.</p></p>
