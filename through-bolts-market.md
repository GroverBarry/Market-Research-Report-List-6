<p><p>Through Bolts are commonly categorized into half screw and full screw bolts based on the type of thread on the shank. Half screw bolts have threads covering only half the length of the shank, while full screw bolts have threads covering the entire length. Half screw bolts are typically used when a smaller portion of the bolt needs to be secured, while full screw bolts are preferred for applications requiring a stronger and more secure fastening option. Both types are widely available in the market to cater to different needs.</p></p>
