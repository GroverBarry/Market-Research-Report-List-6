<p><p>Skin substitutes market can be categorized into different types - acellular, cellular allogeneic, cellular autologous, and others. Acellular skin substitutes are derived from human or animal tissue and lack cells, providing a scaffold for tissue regeneration. Cellular allogeneic substitutes use cells from a different individual of the same species, while cellular autologous substitutes use a patient's own cells. Other types of skin substitutes may include synthetic materials or a combination of different components to promote wound healing and skin regeneration.</p></p>
